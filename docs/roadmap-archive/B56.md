Original item:

The climate cards' valve-opening percentage is wired to the wrong topic on the reporter's TRVs: discovery stamps **`VALVE_STATE`**, while the device's actual percentage datapoint is **`VALVE`** (user-observed on the real system).

**Code ground:** the hm climate recognizer wires the valve from `VALVE_STATE` (preferred, loop `break`s on it) or `LEVEL` as the fallback; `VALVE` is not in the datapoint whitelist at all, so it is never even accumulated.

**Why blind preference for VALVE_STATE is doubly wrong:** `VALVE_STATE` is only a percentage on **classic BidCoS** (HM-CC-RT-DN, 0‚Äì100). On **HmIP** TRVs, where present, `VALVE_STATE` is an **adaptation-status enum** (RUN_TO_START / ADAPTION_IN_PROGRESS / TOO_TIGHT / ‚Ä¶) ‚Äî wiring the percentage to it produces garbage; the HmIP percentage lives in `LEVEL` (0.0‚Äì1.0) or, per the report, in a **`VALVE`** datapoint on newer devices/firmware. A device exposing both the enum and a real percentage currently gets the enum (the `break`).

**Fix direction ‚Äî trust the metadata, not the name:**
1. Add `VALVE` to the whitelist and the wiring candidates.
2. Don't hard-prefer by name: the `hm` payload metadata carries **`datapointMin`/`datapointMax`** ‚Äî a percentage datapoint has max 100 (or 1.0), the HmIP enum has max ~8. Gate valve wiring on a plausible range: prefer `VALVE` (when present, range-validated) ‚Üí `VALVE_STATE` **only if** its max is 100 ‚Üí `LEVEL` (max 1). Stamp `valve_max` from the actual `datapointMax` instead of the current name-based 1/100 guess.
3. **Verify on the reporter's system before finalizing:** which device type reports `VALVE` (eTRV-2/eTRV-E generation?), and its `datapointMax` from the `hm` metadata ‚Äî the range-gating design depends on it.

**Acceptance:** the valve percentage on all three climate cards tracks the real valve opening on BidCoS (VALVE_STATE 0‚Äì100), classic HmIP (LEVEL 0‚Äì1) AND the reporter's `VALVE` devices; a device exposing the HmIP `VALVE_STATE` enum never has its percentage wired to it; existing correctly-wired devices re-discover unchanged.
